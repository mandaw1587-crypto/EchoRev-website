The Sales Scripts

Every script is built around the psychological triggers. Use them for cold DMs, emails, discovery calls, and closing. Swap in your pillar where noted.

1

Cold DM / Cold Email

First touch outreach
Reciprocity Curiosity Specificity
Template  ·  Cold Outreach
Hey [First Name], Noticed you're doing [specific thing you genuinely observed about them]. Most people in your position are leaving money on the table because [specific pain point — e.g., "they're creating content manually when AI can generate a week of it in 90 minutes"]. I put together a quick [3-step checklist / 5-minute Loom / short doc] that shows exactly how to fix that — no pitch, just the thing itself. Want me to send it over? — [Your name]
Why it works: Opens with proof you did your homework (specificity = trust). Leads with value before asking for anything (reciprocity). Ends with a micro-commitment question — a small yes that primes them for a bigger yes. Keep it under 80 words.
2

Follow-Up Message

For no-replies after 2–3 days
Scarcity Social Proof
Template  ·  Follow-Up
Hey [First Name] — just bumping this up. Sent this to a few people in [their niche/industry] this week. [Name] used it to [specific result — e.g., "cut their content workflow from 6 hours to 45 minutes"]. Wanted to make sure you didn't miss it before I close the list. Still happy to send it over if useful. — [Your name]
Why it works: Social proof from a named peer in their niche (not a generic "my clients"). Artificial urgency with "close the list." One line of empathy — you're not pestering, you're providing.
3

Discovery Call Opener

First 3 minutes of the call
Authority Commitment Liking
Script  ·  Call Opener
YOU: "Appreciate you taking the time — I know your calendar's probably packed. Quick thing before we dive in: I want to make sure this call is actually valuable for you either way, so I'm just going to ask some honest questions, and if I don't think I can genuinely help, I'll tell you straight. Sound good?" [They say yes — this is commitment #1] YOU: "Perfect. So tell me — what made you say yes to this call today? What's the thing that's actually keeping you stuck right now?" [Let them talk. Don't interrupt. Take notes.] YOU (after they finish): "Got it. How long has that been the situation?" [They answer] YOU: "And what happens to [their business/income/goal] if that doesn't change in the next 6 months?" [This is the pain amplification question. Let it land. Silence is okay.]
Why it works: The permission frame at the start positions you as the opposite of a pushy salesperson (liking + trust). The commitment question locks in reciprocal engagement. Pain amplification questions make the cost of inaction real — which is the only thing that moves someone off the fence.
4

The Close

After discovery, before they think it over
Urgency Commitment Authority
Script  ·  Closing
YOU (after presenting your offer): "Based on everything you've told me, here's what I'd do for you: [Pillar-specific offer — e.g., 'I'll build you a complete automation workflow that handles X, Y, and Z — so you never have to touch that manually again.'] The investment is [price]. I typically work with [2–3] people at a time so I can actually deliver results instead of just taking money. One of those spots is open right now. Does this feel like the right move for you?" [Wait. Do not fill the silence.] IF HESITATION: "What would need to be true for this to feel like a no-brainer?" [Listen. Address that specific thing only.] IF THEY SAY YES: "Perfect. I'll send over the agreement and onboarding doc tonight. What email should I use?"
Why it works: "I typically work with 2–3 people" is genuine scarcity framing — not fake. The silence after "does this feel right" is intentional. The "what would need to be true" question surfaces the real objection instead of guessing at it.
5

Objection Handlers

The four you'll hear on every call

I need to think about it.

"Totally respect that. Out of curiosity — what specifically do you need to think through? Is it the investment, the timing, or something about whether this will actually work for your situation?" [They'll tell you the real objection. Handle that one, not the decoy.]

It's too expensive.

"I hear you. Let me ask — if this gets you [specific result they said they wanted], what's that worth to your business over the next 12 months? [Pause] If the number you're thinking is more than [your price], then the question isn't whether it's expensive — it's whether you believe it works. So let's talk about that."

I've tried things like this before and they didn't work.

"That makes complete sense — most of what's out there is tactics without the underlying psychology. Can I ask, when you tried [previous thing], what was the part that failed? Was it the strategy, the execution, or did you just not have enough time to commit?" [Listen, then show specifically how your approach addresses that exact failure point.]

I need to talk to my partner / spouse first.

"Of course. What I'd suggest is — would it be easier if we did a quick 15-minute call with both of you so they can ask any questions directly? That way you're not playing telephone and nothing gets lost. If that doesn't work, what would you need from me to make it easier to walk them through it?"

Content Hook Scripts

Muse Media — first 3 seconds of video / opening line of post
Curiosity Pattern Interrupt Identity
5 Hook Templates  ·  Short-Form
// CURIOSITY GAP "Nobody talks about this because the people who know it are too busy making money from it." // IDENTITY ATTACK "If you've been online for more than a year and still haven't made your first $1k — this is why." // SPECIFICITY HOOK "I made $[amount] in [timeframe] using [specific method]. Here's every step, nothing held back." // PATTERN INTERRUPT "Stop posting content. Do this instead." // AUTHORITY HOOK "I've helped [X] people start earning online. The ones who failed all made the same 3 mistakes."
Rule: Every hook must do one of three things — create a gap they need to close, speak to an identity they hold or want, or make a specific claim they don't believe until they watch. If your hook does none of these, rewrite it.
